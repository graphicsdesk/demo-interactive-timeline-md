New Student Orientation Program: August 26th - September 1st

Regardless of which college you are a part of, if you’re a new student you will have to attend the orientation program — more commonly known as NSOP. This week-long program will differ depending on your specific college and the group you’re in. However, for the most part, this week will encompass getting to know the campus, programs at your school, being able to talk to older students, and maybe even getting to know New York City.

End of NSOP

First Day of Classes: Tuesday, September 3rd

The first day of classes will follow memorial day, September 2nd. This officially starts not only the fall semester, but also the shopping period. While the first day is momentous in terms of starting a whole new chapter, your class schedule will follow your normal Tuesday class schedule.

End of shopping period

Last Date to drop Classes: October 8th

While shopping period ends after two weeks of classes, you have almost an additional month to decide whether or not you definitely want to drop a class. Until this date, you are able to freely drop a class without it showing up on your transcript, although you may need permission from your advisor to drop the course.

Fall Midterm Date: October 17th

Columbia University has dubbed October 17th as the official midterm date. Unfortunately, you’ll soon find out that midterms are not just one day or even one month. Many classes have multiple midterms and it can feel like midterm season is months long — and sometimes it is.

Homecoming Game: October 26th

Sometimes it can feel like Columbia is a school that is all about academics, lacking the school spirit that other schools have. Columbia’s football team's Homecoming game is one occasion where a majority of the student body can come together and celebrate the school we go to, try to replicate a tailgate, and cheer for our fellow Lions— win or lose. All it takes to partake in this honored Columbia tradition is a trip to Bakers stadium and some Pantone 292 clothes or Columbia merch.

No Class: November 4th and November 5th

Monday, November 4th is an academic holiday, and November 5th is a holiday in honor of election day. These consecutive days off make it so there’s a four day weekend, five-day if you don’t have Friday classes. Being the first official days off of the fall semester students can elect to visit home, take a well deserved break, or finally visit those New York spots they’ve been putting off.

Election day holiday end

Last day to withdraw or PDF classes: November 14th

While the drop deadline for the semester is on October 8th, if you still are worried about a course you have until the 14th to withdraw or PDF this course. Withdrawing a course means that you won’t finish it, however, it will end up on your transcript as a “W.” You also have the option to PDF a course, which means if you get a letter grade of an A, B, or C, the grade will simply show up as a P. If you get a D or an F, this grade will show up on your transcript. Across all colleges you can elect to PDF one course each semester.

End Thanksgiving Break

Last Day of Classes: December 9th

Reading Period: December 10th - December 12th

Following the last day of classes, there is a short break for a “reading period”. At this time there is no class in attendance, so students are able to study and prepare for their finals and end of semester projects. Many classes will continue to offer office hours and many students may find themselves spending day and night in the libraries in anticipation of the final stretch of the semester.

End reading period
